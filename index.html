What I do.

I help companies build products that are useful and satisfying to use, intuitive, engaging and seamless. My contribution supports long-lasting business value by addressing customer acquisition, activation, retention, and referral.

How.

I join the dots between business objectives, customer expectations, and technical feasibility.

Building successful products requires a deep understanding of user needs and context. I coordinate thorough user research to provide insights that guide product strategy and development. To foster a holistic approach to product creation, I help companies establish lean and agile workflows, streamlining communication between teams.

I empower my teams with a sense of ownership (hence, motivation), control (hence, responsibility), cultivating a culture where people feel safe to take risks and learn from mistakes quickly, maximising the quality of the outcomes.

I keep companies focused on their customers.

Why.

The two things I like about my line of work are challenges and healthy sleep.

Challenges motivate me. Not only in work but in life too. I make active choices, I change things, I lead, and I create rather than react.

Some designers build beautiful interfaces. I don't. I solve problems.

By finding a balance between business objectives and customer needs, I ensure the product's usability and utility. Sometimes it might involve beautiful interfaces, among other things.

I'm comfortable in the knowledge that my work makes a tiny part of somebody's life better. I steer away from dark design patterns, and I sleep well at night.

Experience & Expertise

Design + Business

In my career, I did get exposed to the corporate culture (which was useful knowledge), but I matured as a Designer in the sink-or-swim start-up environment in Shoreditch, London, UK.

Increasing product utility, optimising conversion funnels, improving retention and referral, cutting development costs. Design is a function that adds value to the business.

User-centricity

The Design Thinking we call it. Fancy. We, humans, love coming up with names for things. But to know the word is one thing, but to walk the walk…

Focusing on the customer is important for a business, especially in the long term. And it is important that everyone in the company understands that, not only the people in Design.

I know how to ensure that.

Strategy

Back in 1997 one successful businessperson said, “You’ve got to start with the customer experience and work backwards to the technology”. It’s been almost thirty years now, and it's evident that the approach has been quite successful, yet still lots of businesses ignore that concept in one way or another.

User research is instrumental in shaping the “cohesive larger vision” for a successful product. User testing and iterative design help to maximise the quality of what goes into development. Measuring the existing product informs the ways to optimise it. And the cycle never stops.

Synergy

Cross-functional collaboration is essentially communication. In a small start-up of five people, a CEO, a front- and a back-end engineer, a designer and a marketer all sit around one table every day and collaborate with minimum obstacles - minimum loss of information in the communication.

As the company scales up, we start talking about departments, with heads, and workflows, and politics. But the idea remains: it is still about effective communication. Whether it is a comprehensive workflow or it is small project-based cross-functional teams, the goal is the same: to collaborate, to share expertise, feedback and ideas, to maximise the quality of the outcome, and to minimise the time to get there.

Leadership

I have learnt how to build and how to lead teams through what you might call “blood, sweat, and tears”. And success, and laughter, and conflict, and respect.

I know how to motivate my team, how to give them a sense of ownership and understanding of the impact of their work on the business. I know how to organise the workflow and how to fuse the team with one clear vision.
