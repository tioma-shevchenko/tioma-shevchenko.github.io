What I do.

I help companies build products their customers like using, so much so that they keep coming back. And recommend it to their friends, too. I help with customer acquisition, activation, retention, and referral.

How.

I join the dots: business goals, customer needs and context, technical opportunities and limitations.

To build a product that people need and/or want, first you need to know what those people need and/or want, and you need to know it well. User research provides insights and facilitates informed decision-making.

When you know what you want to build, you need to build it, ideally in the most efficient way, eliminating waste. For that, good communication between Research and Design, Product, Content and SEO, Engineering, Marketing, Sales and the business is needed.

I help companies set up agile and lean workflows that help with the things above.

I empower my teams with a sense of ownership (hence, motivation), control (hence, responsibility), and a culture of psychological safety, where people feel free to take risks, learn from mistakes (hence, maximising the quality of the outcomes).

I keep companies focused on their customers.

Why.

I like two things about my line of work. The first one is a challenge. The second one is healthy sleep.

Challenges motivate me. Not only in work but in life too. I make active choices, I change things, I lead, and I create rather than react. I voice my opinion and stand my ground on important matters.

Some designers build beautiful interfaces. I don't. I solve problems.

I find a balance between what the business needs and what customers need. I ensure the product's usability and utility. Sometimes it might involve beautiful interfaces, among other things.

I'm comfortable in the knowledge that my work makes a tiny part of somebody's life better. I steer away from dark design patterns, and I sleep well at night.

Design + Business

I know what UX Design brings to the business. I know why businesses hire UX professionals. I know why start-ups do, and why big corporations do.

Increasing product utility, optimising conversion funnels, improving retention and referral, cutting development costs. Design is a function that adds value to the business.

Strategy

Back in 1997 one successful businessperson said, “You’ve got to start with the customer experience and work backwards to the technology”. It’s been almost thirty years now, and it's evident that the approach has been quite successful, yet still lots of businesses ignore that concept in one way or another.

User research is instrumental in shaping the “cohesive larger vision” for a successful product. User testing and iterative design help to maximise the quality of what goes into development. Measuring the existing product informs the ways to optimise it. And the cycle never stops.

Synergy

Cross-functional collaboration is essentially communication. In a small start-up of five people, a CEO, a front- and a back-end engineer, a designer and a marketer all sit around one table every day and collaborate with minimum obstacles - minimum loss of information in the communication.

As the company scales up, we start talking about departments, with heads, and workflows, and politics. But the idea remains: it is still about effective communication. Whether it is a comprehensive workflow or it is small project-based cross-functional teams, the goal is the same: to collaborate, to share expertise, feedback and ideas, to maximise the quality of the outcome, and to minimise the time to get there.
